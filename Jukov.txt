Georgy Konstantinovich Zhukov (Russian: Георгий Константинович Жуков, IPA: [ɡʲɪˈorɡʲɪj kənstɐnʲˈtʲinəvʲɪtɕ ˈʐukəf] (listen); 
1 December 1896 – 18 June 1974) was a Soviet general and Marshal of the Soviet Union. He also served as Chief of the General
Staff, Minister of Defence, and was a member of the Presidium of the Communist Party (later Politburo). During World War II,
Zhukov oversaw some of the Red Army's most decisive victories.
