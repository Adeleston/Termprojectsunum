Mao Zedong[a] (26 December 1893 – 9 September 1976), also known as Chairman Mao, was a Chinese communist revolutionary who was the
founder of the People's Republic of China (PRC), which he led as the chairman of the Chinese Communist Party from the establishment
of the PRC in 1949 until his death in 1976. Ideologically a Marxist–Leninist, his theories, military strategies, and political 
policies are collectively known as Maoism.
